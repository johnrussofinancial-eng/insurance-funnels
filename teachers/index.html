Questions Teachers Ask Us

Does my district already provide enough coverage?
Most districts offer 1x your annual salary in group life insurance. For a teacher earning $55,000, that's $55,000 — the industry recommendation for a family with dependents is 10–12x salary. There's almost always a significant gap.
Can I get coverage without a medical exam?
Yes. Several carriers offer simplified issue life insurance up to $500,000 with no medical exam required. We'll identify the best no-exam options based on your age and health situation.
How does an IUL work alongside my pension?
Your pension is taxable income in retirement. An IUL creates a second bucket of tax-free income you can draw from without pushing yourself into a higher tax bracket — giving you significantly more flexibility and net income in retirement.
I'm early in my career — is it worth doing now?
Absolutely — the earlier the better. Life insurance premiums are lowest when you're young and healthy. An IUL started at 28 creates dramatically more tax-free wealth than one started at 45. Time is your biggest advantage.
Are you affiliated with my school district or union?
No. We are completely independent. We are not affiliated with any school district, teacher's union, or state pension system. We work for you — not your employer — and our recommendations are based solely on what's best for your family.
What does the free review actually cover?
In 30 minutes we review your current district coverage, your state pension details, your family situation, and your retirement timeline. You'll leave knowing exactly what gaps exist and what it costs to close them — with zero obligation to buy anything.
